A* and greedy-best-first Comparison

Of particular interest, is the comparison of A* and greedy-best-first. A* is guaranteed to find the optimal path, while greedy-best-first does not have this characteristic, instead sacrificing path optimality for increased iteration efficiency. This can be seen in multiple goal configurations shown.

The same type of relationship is found with BFS and DFS. BFS is a compete algorithm and is guaranteed to find the optimal path, but does so by completely filling the nodes, thus making it have many iterations. On the other hand, DFS is not guaranteed to be optimal, and in many cases, it produces an extremely long tree length. DFS can finish faster in many cases that the other algorithms, but the cost is often an extremely sub-optimal path length.
